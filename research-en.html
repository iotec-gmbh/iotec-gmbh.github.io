OptiBlend

since december 2016

The aim of this project is the development of smartphone-based optical methods for the characterization of single components in the production of blended mineral fertilizers and the derivation of spreading properties for the optimized spreading adjustment of centrifugal spreaders in cooperation with the companies AMAZONEN-Werke H. Dreyer GmbH & Co. KG.

The project "Smartphone based optical methods for characterisation of single components in the production of mineral mixed fertilizers and derivation of spreading properties for optimised spreading settings in centrifugal spreaders" aims to improve the use of mechanically mixed fertilizers (mixed fertilizers) in agricultural practice.
iotec develops machine learning methods for the optical characterization of single and mixed fertilizers on photos. A database is created from thousands of photos of fertilizer grains and their characteristics and a neural net is trained.

The future of horticulture

15.08.2016

Mobile soil sampling laboratory for a more efficient use of resources

The University of Applied Sciences in Osnabrueck and its project partners (including iotec) received funding totalling more than 1.3 million euros from the Federal Ministry of Food and Agriculture for the "soil2data" research project. The project is scheduled to run from 2016 to 2019.
In the "soil2data" project, a mobile soil sensor module for carrier vehicles is being developed that takes soil samples while driving over an agricultural area and analyzes the soil properties in-situ.
Until now , a large number of soil samples have been collected for soil analysis and are then sent to a laboratory for evaluation. This means a high manual effort as well as a high delay until the results are available.
The research team is working on evaluating the results of this soil analysis directly in the field. To put it simply: "We leave the soil in the field, because we only want the data," explained Prof. Dr. Arno Ruckelshausen during the presentation of the project.

ONYX MiO – Ultralight Organic-Hybrid-Mobile

since december 2015

The Companies ONYX composites GmbH and iotec GmbH are developing an ultralight bio-hybrid mobile. The development of the feasibility study is being funded by the DBU.
The hybrid vehicle should be able to cover short to medium distances safely, comfortably, health-promoting and, above all, exhaust emission-free, thanks to the mix of an electric motor and pedal power. Thanks to a range of 30 to 80 kilometers, a range of up to 85 kilometers per hour (L7e variant) and a weight of less than 180 kilograms (without battery), the two-seater could be used wherever the bicycle reaches both range and comfort limits. A clever combination of fibre composites with technically proven components from the field of electric bicycle construction is what makes this vehicle so light and environmentally friendly that it resembles a recumbent bike with bodywork. With a low entrance, two small front wheels and two large rear wheels as well as removable windows and doors, it offers sufficient space for two people to sit next to each other. The functional capability of the concept has already been proven in initial driving tests, and a realistic prototype is now to be created in the next development step.
