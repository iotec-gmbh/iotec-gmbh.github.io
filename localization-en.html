Indoor localization is the determination of the position of objects or people in buildings. Since GPS does not function reliably indoors due to the lack of visual contact with the GPS satellites, it is necessary to use a system that uses other technologies. For this purpose, WLAN, Bluetooth, UWB (UltrawideBand) or RFID (Radio-frequency Identification) can be used. Ultra-wideband is a short-range radio technology with an accuracy of less than 30cm and is therefore significantly higher than that of Bluetooth or WLAN. Differences in height can also be determined very accurately. UWB is based on the Time of Flight (ToF) runtime procedure. The light propagation time between an object and several receivers (anchors) is measured. For the exact localization of an object at least 3 receivers are necessary (trilateration).
